The Giants of The East

Taraba State is home to the highest peaks in West Africa. Explore a land of towering mountains, rare biodiversity, and landscapes that defy imagination.

The Mambilla Plateau

Walk in the Clouds. Standing at an average elevation of 1,600 meters, this is Nigeria's coldest region. Experience the rolling green hills, endless tea plantations, and winding roads that look more like Switzerland than West Africa. It is the only place in Nigeria where tea grows naturally.

Gashaka-Gumti National Park

The Wilderness Awaits. As Nigeria’s largest national park, Gashaka-Gumti is a biodiversity hotspot. It is home to chimpanzees, leopards, and rare bird species. The park offers a rugged landscape of savannahs, forests, and crystal-clear rivers perfect for the true explorer.

Chappal Waddi (The Mountain of Death)

Conquer the Peak. Dare to climb the highest point in Nigeria? Standing tall at 2,419 meters, Chappal Waddi offers the toughest and most rewarding trek in West Africa. The view from the top is a breathtaking panorama of Nigeria and the Cameroonian border.

Flavors & Rhythms of the Highlands

From the fiery spices of our kitchens to the thunderous beats of our festivals, experience the soul of Taraba.

A Symphony of Spice

In the Rolling Hills, food is a love language. Our cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of indigenous flavors. Experience the world-famous Nigerian Jollof Rice, a smoky, tomato-rich masterpiece served with golden fried plantains (Dodo) and peppered chicken.

Rhythms of the Ancestors

The culture of the Rolling Hills is as enduring as the mountains themselves. Witness the Mambilla Fishing Festival or the harvest celebrations, where intricate masquerades and thunderous drumming tell stories of our heritage and hospitality.
